Onion Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups of chicken or beef broth
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 tablespoon of unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4-6 slices of crusty bread
  • 1 cup of grated Gruyere cheese

Instructions:

  1. Heat the butter and olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until soft and caramelized, about 20-30 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic to the saucepan and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  3. Pour in the broth and water and bring to a simmer. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Preheat the broiler. Place slices of bread on a baking sheet and top each slice with a generous amount of grated Gruyere cheese. Broil until the cheese is melted and bubbly, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Ladle the hot soup into oven-safe bowls and top each bowl with a slice of cheesy bread. Broil until the cheese is melted and golden brown, about 2-3 minutes.
  6. Serve the soup hot and enjoy.

Here are five recipes for salting herring: Classic salted herring Ingredients: 1 lb. fresh herring 1 cup salt 1 cup sugar 3 cups water 2 bay leaves 2 cloves of garlic, minced 2 tbsp. white wine vinegar 2 tbsp. olive oil 1 tsp. peppercorns Instructions: Clean the herring and remove the heads, tails and gut. Cut the fish into small fillets. In a saucepan, bring water, salt, sugar, bay leaves, garlic, white wine vinegar, and peppercorns to a boil. Let the brine cool down to room temperature. Place the herring fillets in a large, non-reactive container and cover with the brine. Cover the container with a lid or plastic wrap and let it sit in the refrigerator for 24 hours. Drain the herring and gently pat it dry with paper towels. Drizzle with olive oil and serve with bread or crackers. Pickled herring Ingredients: 1 lb. fresh herring 1 cup white wine vinegar 1 cup water 1/2 cup sugar 1 onion, sliced 1 tbsp. salt 2 bay leaves 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 tsp. peppercorns Instructions: Clean the herring and remove the heads, tails and gut. Cut the fish into small fillets. In a saucepan, bring the white wine vinegar, water, sugar, onion, salt, bay leaves, garlic, and peppercorns to a boil. Let the pickling liquid cool down to room temperature. Place the herring fillets in a large, non-reactive container and cover with the pickling liquid. Cover the container with a lid or plastic wrap and let it sit in the refrigerator for 24 hours. Drain the herring and serve with bread or crackers. Herring in sour cream Ingredients: 1 lb. salted herring 1 cup sour cream 1/2 onion, finely chopped 2 tbsp. freshly chopped parsley 2 tbsp. freshly chopped dill 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 tsp. sugar 1 tsp. salt Instructions: Soak the salted herring in cold water for several hours to remove excess salt. Drain the herring and cut it into small pieces. In a bowl, mix together the sour cream, onion, parsley, dill, garlic, sugar, and salt. Add the herring pieces to the bowl and stir until they are coated with the sour cream mixture. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it sit in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours. Serve the herring in sour cream with bread or crackers.
